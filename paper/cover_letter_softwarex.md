Dear Editor,

I am submitting the above manuscript for consideration as an Original Software Publication in *SoftwareX*.

Chemical safety practitioners in Korea must consult up to six separate government portals to evaluate a single substance — a fragmented workflow taking ~150 minutes per inquiry. **ChemIP** addresses this by unifying nine public data sources (KOSHA, KIPRIS, KOTRA, MFDS, OpenFDA, KISCHEM, NCIS, PubMed, Naver) into a single-query platform with local LLM-assisted evidence synthesis.

**Why SoftwareX:**

1. **Reuse potential** — Fully open-source (AGPL-3.0), 19 automated tests, CI/CD pipeline, single-command installation.
2. **Novel integration** — First open-source platform combining MSDS, multi-jurisdiction patent indexing (350M+ records, 6 patent offices), trade intelligence, drug cross-reference, and LLM analysis.
3. **Demonstrated impact** — 78% reduction in per-substance investigation time across three controlled scenario evaluations.
4. **Technical depth** — Aho-Corasick multi-pattern matching at scale, multi-criteria guide recommendation with deterministic ranking, dual-path AI analysis with hallucination guards.

Source code: https://github.com/yuyongkim/chemIP

This manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under consideration by any other journal. No conflicts of interest.
